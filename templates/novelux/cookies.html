Cookie Policy

This policy explains what cookies are, which ones Novelux uses, and how you can control them.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and understand how you interact with content.

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the platform to function. Without them, you cannot log in or use core features. They cannot be disabled.

Functional Cookies

These cookies remember your preferences so you don't have to reset them on every visit — things like dark mode, font size, and your reading position in a chapter. You can disable them, but your preferences won't be saved.

Analytics Cookies

We use anonymised analytics to understand how readers navigate the platform, which features are popular, and where improvements are needed. This data is aggregated and never tied to your personal identity.

You can opt out of analytics cookies at any time in your account settings under Privacy Preferences.

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services (such as payment processors) that set their own cookies. We do not control these cookies — please refer to the respective third party's cookie policy for details.

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View which cookies are stored
  • Delete cookies at any time
  • Block all or certain types of cookies

Note that blocking essential cookies will prevent you from logging in or using the platform properly.
