Ch. 12 — The Reckoning

Bound by Blood · Adaeze M. · 4,821 words · Submitted 3h ago

The rain had not stopped for three days. Kemi stood at the edge of the veranda, watching the red earth turn to mud, and she wondered if the gods were weeping too.

"You should come inside," Auntie Bose called from somewhere in the dark house. "The rain will not wash away your troubles."

But Kemi was not so sure. She had been carrying this weight for twelve long years, and she had learned that sometimes the rain was the only honest thing left in the world.

She turned back inside, her wrapper damp at the hem. The parlour smelled of eba egusi soup and kerosene, familiar smells that used to mean safety.

Tomorrow, everything would change. Chidi was coming home.
