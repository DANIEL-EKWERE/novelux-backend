Editorial Guidelines

Master policy document — CE authority.

AE Scope — What AEs Can Do

  • ✓ Review and annotate submitted chapters
  • ✓ Approve chapters that clearly meet guidelines
  • ✓ Request revisions with structured feedback to authors
  • ✓ Flag content for SE review
  • ✗ Cannot remove content — must escalate to SE
  • ✗ Cannot make policy decisions
  • ✗ Cannot suspend or action author accounts

SE Scope — What SEs Can Do

  • ✓ All AE capabilities
  • ✓ Review and decide on AE-escalated content
  • ✓ Remove content that clearly violates policy
  • ✓ Issue major revision requests
  • ✓ Monitor AE team performance
  • ✓ Propose policy changes to CE
  • ✗ Cannot approve policy changes — CE only
  • ✗ Cannot permanently suspend author accounts — CE only
  • ✗ Ambiguous or novel cases must be escalated to CE

CE Scope — Chief Editor Authority

  • ✓ All SE capabilities
  • ✓ Final authority on all escalated decisions
  • ✓ Approve, amend, or reject policy changes
  • ✓ Permanently suspend or reinstate author accounts
  • ✓ Hire, promote, or remove editorial team members
  • ✓ Set editorial precedent for novel content types
  • ✓ Platform-wide content health oversight
